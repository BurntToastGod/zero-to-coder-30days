## 🧠 Concept: Print & Variables

`print()` tells Python to show something on screen.
A **variable** stores a piece of information with a name.

```python
print("Hello, world!")
name = "Alex"
print(name)
```

- Line 1: Shows the text `Hello, world!`
- Line 2: Creates a variable called `name` and stores `"Alex"` in it
- Line 3: Shows whatever is inside `name`

2. Delete the `=` sign
3. Change `print` to `Print` (capital P)
